MAC Addressing

A MAC address (media access control address) is the unique identifier your home router sees your device as. Using the MAC address, routers can direct packets to the right computer based on the information on the header of a packet.

Networking Hardware

The most common kind of networking hardware is the ethernet interface. It allows the connection of an ethernet wire to the computer and access to the internet. Most modern computers now support wireless connections also known as Wi-Fi through a Wi-Fi radio, however, the Ethernet interface remains the fastest and most reliable option, especially for important applications.

Additionally, another piece of networking hardware most are familiar with is a router. Most homes have a router to allow multiple devices to connect to the internet at once without issues. The router is crucial because it takes care of assigning a unique internal address to each connected device to differentiate each of them, and also takes care of forwarding the right packets to the right device. For example, if an email gets sent to your phone, the router will redirect the packets to your phoneâ€™s address and not your laptop. If you request to load the page YouTube on your laptop, the router will send the data to your laptop.

Another important piece of networking hardware is a modem. Modems allows computers to send and receive data through an analog telephone communication line, cable, or satellite. The purpose of the modem is to convert analog signal from the telephone line to a digital signal which the computer will be able to understand, and vice versa for outgoing data.
